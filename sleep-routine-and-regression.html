How Does a Sleep Routine Help a Baby with a Sleep Regression?

Published on: July 2026

Sleep regressions are a normal stage of baby development. During this period, it's common for a baby who was sleeping well to start waking up several times at night, take longer to fall asleep, or take shorter naps. Although it's impossible to completely avoid a regression, establishing a consistent sleep routine can reduce its impact and help your baby recover a quality rest sooner.

Why is a routine important during a sleep regression?

A sleep routine gives your baby clear signals that it's time to rest. By repeating the same activities every night, the brain learns to associate them with sleep, making it easier for the baby to relax.

A well-established routine helps to:

  • Create a predictable and safe environment.
  • Reduce overstimulation before bedtime.
  • Promote relaxation and natural melatonin production.
  • Make it easier for the baby to fall back asleep after night awakenings.
  • Maintain healthy habits even during the most difficult stages.

Even though awakenings may continue during the regression, a consistent routine usually helps the baby face this period with greater tranquility.

How to create a good sleep routine?

It doesn't need to be complicated. The important thing is to always maintain the same order of activities. A routine can include:

  • Lowering the intensity of the lights.
  • A relaxing bath.
  • Putting on pajamas.
  • The last feeding before sleep.
  • Reading a story or singing a lullaby.
  • Creating a calm environment with relaxing sounds or white noise.
  • Putting the baby to bed when they are drowsy.

Consistency is the most important factor. Repeating this sequence every day helps the baby anticipate that it's time to sleep.
